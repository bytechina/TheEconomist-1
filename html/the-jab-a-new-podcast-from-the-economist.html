Introducing The Jab
The Jab—a new podcast from The Economist

The sharp end of the global vaccination race

Economist Radio
Podcasts

90-YEAR-OLD Margaret Keenan from Coventry in England was the first person in the world to receive the jab for covid-19 in December 2020. More than a hundred million shots have been administered since then, but the number of cases of covid-19 detected worldwide has also passed the hundred million mark. We’re in a race between infections and injections.

"The Jab from Economist Radio" is a new podcast reporting from the sharp end of the vaccination race. The Economist’s science and data teams, a global network of correspondents, and interviews with key players bring a unique perspective and depth of insight to the challenge of a generation.

As this race enters its most crucial phase we’ll keep you up to date with the latest developments and unpick the complex process that takes vaccines from the lab and into the arms of the people who need it.

The stakes couldn’t be higher. It’s the most ambitious inoculation programme the world has ever seen and the health and wealth of the planet is in the balance.

Each week Alok Jha, The Economist’s science correspondent, hosts with Natasha Loder, our health-policy editor. Each episode tackles a different theme along the supply chain — from the innovations that make the new vaccines possible and the politics of distribution, to the drivers of vaccine hesitancy.
